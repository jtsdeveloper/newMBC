I'm New

Welcome to Maranatha Baptist Church! All are welcome to worship and serve in this loving, faithful church community. We understand that you may have some questions prior to visiting with us, so read the following section and we'll do our best to let you know what to expect.

What is your weekly schedule?

Every Sunday morning, we have Sunday School beginning at 10:00am with worship beginning at 11:00am. On most Sundays we hold an evening worship service or Bible study at 6:00pm. On Wednesday evenings, we have a devotion and prayer meeting beginning at 6:30 with choir rehearsal following at 7:15

Is there a nursery or childcare?

Maranatha offers childcare for infants and toddlers during Sunday School and worship. Our infant area and toddler room are each staffed by two church volunteers. If you have any questions prior to visiting, don’t hesitate to contact us.

How should I dress?

There is no dress code at Maranatha – you are welcome to wear whatever feels most comfortable. On Sunday mornings, a majority of members and guests will be dressed somewhere between “business casual” and “Sunday best,” but several folks will also be wearing blue jeans and a casual top. Either is perfectly acceptable to us.

On Sunday and Wednesday evenings, everyone (including the leaders) is dressed more casually.

What is your worship like?

Most people would classify our worship as "traditional." In the course of our service we sing hymns, say multiple prayers, and participate in responsive readings. The sermon is about 20 minutes long and the entire serviec lasts approximately one hour.

Where are you located?

Maranatha Baptist Church is located at 148 Georgia Highway 45 North, Plains, GA 31780.

About Us

As a Baptist church, we value what Walter Shurden referred to as "The Four Fragile Freedoms" of Baptist identity

Maranatha Baptist Church subscribes to the doctrinal statement of the 1963 Baptist Faith & Message. To read the statement in it's entirety, click here.

It should be noted that local baptist churches are not subject to outside governing bodies or documents. Though we can affirm much, if not all, of what is in this Baptist Faith and Message, we also confess that its contents can be interpreted differently amongst individuals and churches and may not reflect the totality of our beliefs as Christians.
